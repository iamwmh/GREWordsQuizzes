A hands-free, voice-first study companion. Listen to smart clues from several angles, say the word out loud, and let the app check your answer — then it brings each word back for review right when you’re about to forget it.

What makes it different

Built for active recall. You speak the answers, so you practice retrieval the same way you would in a real test of memory.

🗣️

Multi-angle spoken hints

Every word is revealed through different angles — its definition, a vivid description of what it represents, a synonym or antonym, and its first letters. The clue types are mixed in randomly so each round feels fresh.

🎤

Say your answer

After a “beep-beep-beep” cue, the app listens for your spoken English answer and converts it to text to check instantly.

⏱️

Patient, adaptive pacing

No answer in time? You get the next clue. Answer early but wrong? It asks “Another chance, or finish?” — your call.

🧠

On-device hint generation

Apple Intelligence’s on-device model writes fresh, natural clues. When unavailable, curated offline clues keep everything working.

⭐

High-frequency words first

About 2,000 of the most commonly tested GRE words (pulled from Magoosh, Barron’s, Manhattan, GregMat, Powerscore, PrepScholar and more) are flagged and practiced first — so you master what matters most.

📚

12,000-word bank

A large local GRE-level vocabulary with definitions, fully searchable and filterable by high-frequency. Add your own words anytime.

🧠

Spaced-repetition review

Every word you practice is scheduled on the Ebbinghaus forgetting curve. Each day the Review tab resurfaces the words you're about to forget — sooner if you miss one, later as your recall strengthens — plus a day-by-day list of everything you've studied.

📅

Goals & calendar tracking

Plan a daily word goal (default 15) and watch your streak grow on a color-coded study calendar.

How a round works

Everything happens out loud, so you can study with your eyes closed or on the go.

Hint 1 · Meaning clue

The app speaks a clue — a paraphrased definition, a vivid description, or a synonym/antonym (chosen at random) — then plays the “beep-beep-beep” cue and listens for ~3 seconds.

Hint 2 · Another angle

Still stuck? You get a second meaning clue of a different type, so each word is approached from more than one direction.

Hint 3 · Spelling

As a final nudge, it reveals the first two letters and listens one more time for your answer.

Reveal & track

The correct word is shown, spelled out, and spoken. Your result is saved to today’s goal and calendar, and the word is scheduled for spaced-repetition review.

Clues from every angle

Each word can be approached through several kinds of spoken clue. The app mixes them in randomly every round, so you build flexible, well-connected memory instead of memorizing one fixed cue.

📖

Definition

A natural, paraphrased description of what the word means — rewritten on-device by Apple Intelligence when available.

🎨

Characteristics

A vivid image of the thing’s features, typical use, color, shape, or the feeling it evokes.

🔁

Synonym & antonym

A close-in-meaning word (“close in meaning to …”) or its opposite (“roughly the opposite of …”), drawn from WordNet. These appear at random when a word has them, training you to recall the word from related vocabulary — the way the test really probes it.

🔤

Spelling

As a final nudge, the app reveals the first two letters of the word.

Two clues in the same round never repeat each other’s content, so every hint adds something genuinely new.

🧠 Review on the Ebbinghaus forgetting curve

Memory fades on a predictable curve. The app schedules every word you practice so it returns for review right before you’re likely to forget it — the most effective way to move vocabulary into long-term memory.

📈

Smart scheduling

After each answer, a word’s next review is set along expanding intervals — 1, 2, 4, 7, 15, 30, 60, then 120 days. Recall it correctly and the gap grows; miss it and it comes back sooner so you relearn it.

🎲

A fresh batch daily

The Review tab gathers the words that are due today and randomly samples them into a spoken review session — the same listen-and-speak flow as Practice. Choose how many to review and tap start.

🗂️

Your daily word list

Everything you’ve studied is kept in a day-by-day list — Today, Yesterday, and earlier — so you can browse and revisit any past word, and see when each one is next due.

⭐ Focus on the words that are actually tested

Not all GRE words are equal. The app statistically identifies the most commonly tested, high-frequency vocabulary and puts it at the front of your practice queue.

⭐

Flagged automatically

~2,000 high-frequency words are marked across the bank — drawn from well-known GRE lists (Magoosh, Barron’s, Manhattan, GregMat, Powerscore, PrepScholar, Greenlight and more). Look for the star in the Word Bank and filter to see them all.

🎯

Practiced first

Turn on “Focus on high-frequency words” in Practice. Sessions prioritize starred words — and within those, the ones you haven’t mastered yet — so your time goes to the highest-impact vocabulary.

📈

Track your mastery

The Progress tab shows how many high-frequency words you’ve mastered (e.g., 0 / 2,039) with a live progress bar, so you always know how close you are to covering the core list.

Getting started & tips

A few things that help you get the most out of the app.

✅

Allow Microphone & Speech

On first launch the app asks for Microphone and Speech Recognition access. These are required to hear and check your spoken answers.

🔇

Find a quiet spot

Recognition works best with minimal background noise. Speak clearly right after the three beeps.

🎯

Set your daily goal

Open the Progress tab to plan how many words you want per day. Practice sessions are sized to your remaining goal.

➕

Add your own words

Use the “+” in the Word Bank to add a word and definition. The app generates its three clues automatically.

🧠

Apple Intelligence (optional)

On supported devices, enable Apple Intelligence in Settings for freshly written, on-device clues. Otherwise curated clues are used.

🔁

“Another chance or finish?”

If you answer before the last hint and it’s wrong, just say “another chance” to keep going, or “finish” to see the answer.

Private by design

Your word bank and progress are stored locally on your device. There are no accounts, no ads, and no third-party tracking. See the Privacy Policy for details.

FAQ

Quick answers to common questions.

Does it work offline?

Yes. The word bank and curated clues are on-device. Speech recognition and Apple Intelligence may use Apple’s services depending on your device and settings.

Do I need to type?

No typing needed to answer — you speak. You can optionally type to search the Word Bank or add new words.

How does review scheduling work?

Every word you practice is placed on the Ebbinghaus forgetting curve and given a next-review date (1, 2, 4, 7, 15, 30, 60, 120-day intervals). Open the Review tab each day to study the words that are due — correct answers space them further out, misses bring them back sooner.

Where do synonyms and antonyms come from?

They’re drawn from WordNet and bundled in the app, so synonym/antonym clues work entirely offline. They appear at random alongside the other clue types when a word has them.

Which devices are supported?

iPhone and iPad running a recent version of iOS/iPadOS. Apple Intelligence features require a compatible device with the feature enabled.
